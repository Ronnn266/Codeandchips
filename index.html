ABSTRACT

The purpose of this study is to evaluate the service quality and student satisfaction in the school cafeteria at Lyceum of Southern Luzon. The research aims to assess how well the cafeteria meets students' expectations in terms of food quality, hygiene, menu variety, pricing, and overall service. Data were collected through interviews with students from Junior High School (JHS), Senior High School (SHS), and College during recess and lunch hours. The findings reveal several key areas for improvement, including the quality of food, the cleanliness of the cafeteria, food safety practices, and the affordability of meals. Students expressed dissatisfaction with the cafeteria’s food quality, particularly its limited healthy options and pricing, which many found to be too high. Hygiene practices were also a concern, as students noted that staff did not consistently follow food safety protocols, such as wearing gloves when preparing food. Additionally, the cafeteria's seating capacity and overall atmosphere were identified as inadequate, especially during peak hours. Based on these findings, the study recommends improvements in food quality, including offering healthier options, and enhancing hygiene practices by implementing better food storage techniques and staff training. Furthermore, the school should consider restructuring the cafeteria layout to reduce congestion and introducing more affordable meal options to accommodate students with limited budgets. This study aims to provide actionable recommendations to improve the overall student dining experience and contribute to a healthier and more enjoyable environment within the school canteen.

Keywords: Service Quality, Student Satisfaction, Food Quality, Hygiene, Canteen, School Cafeteria, Student Preferences

Background of the Study

In the realm of business and management, service quality stands as one of the cornerstones in providing quality foods with proper service and good hygiene practice to the customer. Service quality signifies how well a food establishment or company caters for its services compared to what customers expect. Customers have specific needs and standards for how they want to be helped. Thus, it is important for businesses to meet these expectations in order to keep customers happy, build a good reputation, and make money.

Service quality in school canteens is vital for enhancing student satisfaction and fostering a healthy environment. Key dimensions of service quality include food quality, hygiene, cleanliness, responsiveness, professionalism, and atmosphere. Canteens should offer fresh, nutritious meals with a variety of options to meet diverse dietary needs. Hygiene practices are crucial; staff must wear gloves when handling food and maintain rigorous cleaning protocols to ensure safety. A clean and inviting dining area contributes to a positive experience, while attentive and courteous staff help address student needs effectively.

By prioritizing these aspects, school canteens can significantly impact students' perceptions and satisfaction, leading to a more enjoyable dining experience and encouraging healthy eating habits. Another critical aspect is cleanliness. The overall environment of the canteen should be well-maintained, with regular cleaning of dining areas, food preparation spaces, and restrooms. A clean and inviting atmosphere not only enhances the dining experience but also reflects the school's commitment to student health and well-being.

Finally, the overall atmosphere of the canteen plays a significant role in student satisfaction. Comfortable seating arrangements, appropriate decor, and a lively, engaging environment can enhance the dining experience, making it a social hub where students want to spend time.

In line with the aforementioned statements, understanding and improving these dimensions of service quality is essential, as they directly influence students' perceptions and satisfaction levels. A high-quality canteen experience not only enhances students' enjoyment of their meals but also encourages healthy eating habits and social interactions. Conversely, inadequate service quality can lead to dissatisfaction, negatively impacting students' dietary choices and overall school moral

In the context of the Philippines, student satisfaction in school canteens hinges on several key factors. First, the quality and nutritional value of food are paramount, as students tend to prefer local dishes like adobo and sinigang. When schools incorporate nutritious options that reflect these preferences, students are more likely to enjoy their meals. Additionally, a diverse menu that includes both traditional and modern choices caters to varying tastes and dietary needs, enhancing satisfaction. Pricing also plays a significant role, as many families operate on tight budgets; affordable meal options can significantly increase canteen usage.

This research aims to provide actionable recommendations for improving the overall student dining experience at the Lyceum of Southern Luzon.
